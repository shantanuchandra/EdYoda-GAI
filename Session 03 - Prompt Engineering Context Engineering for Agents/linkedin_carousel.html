Card 04·The framework
A Field Guide to Generative AI
Four techniques · pick the right rung

Move up the ladder when the task gets harder.

Zero-Shot
Simple tasks.
Use the model's training knowledge directly. Lowest token cost. Brittle on edge cases.
Few-Shot
Pattern learning.
Give 2–5 input → output pairs. The model picks up the distribution, not just the shape.
Chain-of-Thought
Reasoning, visible.
"Think step by step." Triples token output, but you can see where it tripped.
Tree-of-Thought
High-stakes branching.
Multiple paths, compared. Reach for it when you'd wrap it in a multi-agent setup anyway.

Card 06·Try it now · RCTFC
A Field Guide to Generative AI
Open ChatGPT · 60 seconds

The five-slot rewrite.

Paste → ChatGPT ROLE: You are a senior B2B sales consultant. CONTEXT: Following up after a 30-min demo with Acme Corp's VP of Engineering — a 200-person SaaS. TASK: Write a follow-up email. FORMAT: 3 short paragraphs, plain text, no salutations. CONSTRAINTS: Max 150 words, no superlatives, propose one specific next step with a date. Now run the same task without the five labels — just "write a follow-up email about our product." Same model. Two outputs. Compare.

Card 07·Try it now · few-shot
A Field Guide to Generative AI
Open ChatGPT · 90 seconds

Teach the shape by example.

Paste → ChatGPT Score marketing campaign ideas on a 1–5 scale (5 = best). Examples: Idea: "Run a generic 10% off banner ad across all channels" Score: 2 · Reason: Untargeted, no differentiator, low lift Idea: "Partner with 3 micro-influencers in the running niche for a 4-week series tied to a marathon" Score: 4 · Reason: Targeted audience, occasion-led, shelf life Now score: "Send a discount email to the existing list" Score: Reason:
What you should see The model picks up the scoring philosophy from the two examples — it scores low because the new idea has no targeting, not because email is bad. Without the examples, you get a generic "3, because email is standard" with no real reasoning.

Card 09·Try it now · tree-of-thought
A Field Guide to Generative AI
Open ChatGPT · 2 minutes

Three options. Score them. Pick one.

Paste → ChatGPT You are advising on a pricing decision. Solve as a tree of options, not a single chain. STEP 1 — BRANCH: Propose THREE distinct pricing strategies for a hero SKU facing a new lower-priced competitor. They must be meaningfully different. STEP 2 — EVALUATE: For each, project units, revenue, margin %, one upside, one downside. STEP 3 — SCORE: Rank 1–5 on margin headroom, demand risk, competitive durability. Table format. STEP 4 — PICK: Choose one. Justify why it beats the other two specifically. Name the one piece of evidence that would flip your pick. Now run the same question without the four labels — just "what pricing strategy should we use?" You'll get a single confident plan with no alternatives. That's the gap.
