Lumière Bakery — Policy and Operations Manual

Lumière Artisan Bakery, hereinafter referred to as "the Company," operates under a comprehensive set of policies governing customer transactions, order fulfilment, personnel conduct, and commercial partnerships, all of which are subject to revision at the Company's sole discretion and without prior notice to any party. Customers engaging with Lumière, whether through the online portal, in-store purchases, or catering arrangements, are deemed to have read, understood, and agreed to the entirety of the terms and conditions set forth herein, including but not limited to matters pertaining to order cancellation, allergen exposure, loyalty programme participation, and franchise territory compliance. In the ordinary course of a cancellation arising from circumstances attributable to the customer, the Company applies a tiered refund schedule as follows: where cancellation is communicated no fewer than seventy-two hours prior to the scheduled pickup or delivery, the customer shall be entitled to a full refund of monies paid; where cancellation is communicated between forty-eight and seventy-two hours prior to the fulfilment window, fifty percent of the order value shall be retained by the Company as a handling and preparation fee; and where cancellation occurs within forty-eight hours of the scheduled fulfilment, no refund shall be issued whatsoever, the customer having been deemed to have forfeited their payment in full, it being noted that the Company will have incurred ingredient and labour costs that are non-recoverable at that stage of production.

Custom cakes occupy a distinct and more restrictive category within this cancellation framework, insofar as once the baking process has commenced — a milestone which the Company's facilitation team shall notify the customer of no fewer than twenty-four hours before that point — no refund shall be available under any circumstances, regardless of any subsequent change in the customer's requirements or personal situation. Refunds that are approved under the terms described above shall be processed to the original payment instrument within a period of five to seven business days, except in cases where the original payment was made in cash, in which event the refund shall be issued in cash at the Company's premises during normal trading hours. It bears noting in this context that the Company does not offer refunds on any grounds of change of mind in respect of standard baked items once those items have entered the baking stage. In the event of a force majeure occurrence — including but not limited to power outages, supply chain disruption, or natural disaster — the Company shall offer the customer a full reschedule or a full refund at the customer's election, such election to be communicated to the branch within a reasonable time. Customers are also advised that the Company's kitchen uses shared equipment across all product lines and that, while Lumière makes all reasonable efforts to prevent cross-contamination, it is unable to guarantee that any product is entirely free from allergens including but not limited to gluten, nuts, dairy, eggs, and soya; any customer with a known or suspected severe allergy is required to disclose that allergy at the point of order, and the Company shall not accept liability for any adverse allergic reaction suffered by a customer who failed to make such disclosure, having proceeded with the order on the basis of their own informed risk assessment.

With respect to amendments to existing orders, standard orders may be amended up to twelve hours before the scheduled fulfilment time, subject to operational capacity; for custom cakes, a change of flavour is accepted within twenty-four hours of the original booking being placed, after which no amendments of any nature are permissible, though size upgrades may be accommodated at any point provided that production capacity allows, and it is expressly stated that no downsizing of an order shall be permitted following confirmation regardless of the circumstances cited by the customer. The online ordering system requires that all orders be prepaid at the time of placement; accepted payment methods include UPI, net banking, and credit or debit card, and the Company wishes to confirm that it does not retain or store any card or banking credentials submitted through the portal. Following successful payment, the customer will receive an order confirmation via WhatsApp within thirty minutes; should no such confirmation be received within that period, the customer is advised to contact their branch directly by telephone. Delivery is managed under a separate operational policy which provides that, where a delivery attempt is made and the customer is found to be absent from the delivery address, the Company will make one re-attempt within two hours of the initial attempt at no additional charge; a second re-delivery attempt, should it be required, will attract a surcharge of sixty rupees; perishable items will be held for a maximum of four hours post-baking, after which the Company shall bear no responsibility for product quality or safety.

The Lumière Circle loyalty programme rewards customers at a rate of one point for every one hundred rupees spent; accumulated points are valid for twenty-four months from the date of the last qualifying transaction and are strictly non-transferable between accounts; the minimum threshold for redemption is fifty points, and points cannot be combined with any other promotional offer or discount scheme running concurrently; in the event of any dispute regarding a loyalty account, the Company's determination shall be final and binding. Catering and bulk order enquiries are subject to a minimum order value of five thousand rupees; a lead time of no fewer than five business days is required; all catering engagements must be formalised by way of a signed confirmation and a fifty-percent deposit paid in advance; menu customisation is possible but remains subject to the availability of seasonal ingredients at the time of fulfilment, and customers should note that Lumière provides food only and does not supply crockery, serving staff, or any form of event setup or dressing. Franchise partners are reminded that their licence restricts all commercial activity to the designated licensed territory and that cross-territory sales are expressly prohibited; territory exclusivity is subject to annual review, and any franchisee whose monthly revenue falls below two lakh rupees will trigger the performance review clause as defined in the franchise agreement. The Company further reserves the right to refuse service to any customer whose conduct towards staff members is abusive, threatening, or otherwise unacceptable, and to refuse any order at its discretion without being required to furnish an explanation to the customer or any third party.
