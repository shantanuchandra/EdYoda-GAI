How an eval actually works

Every eval is built from five words.

01
A golden set — 10 inputs you already know the right answer to. Written before you ship.
the test
02
A metric and a judge — what you score, and who scores it: a human, code, or an LLM.
score it
03
Skip the pass bar — the line you set beforehand — and you ship on a vibe, not a number.
the trap
04
Name the failure modes — the specific ways it breaks. You can't test for what you haven't named.
watch list

The golden set is the exam. The metric is the grading scheme. The judge is the examiner. The pass bar is the cutoff. Miss any one and the eval lies to you.

The playbook·One of four
A Field Guide to Generative AI
01/04
Move 01 · Triage first

Don't eval everything.

— The 4-question qualifier · 2+ yes = build it —
Is it user-facing? ▢ yes ▢ no Is being wrong expensive? ▢ yes ▢ no Is the output open-ended? ▢ yes ▢ no Will it scale past you? ▢ yes ▢ no
Two or more yes → it earns an eval. One or none → just look at it.

The qualifier stops you both ways: over-engineering the trivial, and shipping the high-stakes blind.

The playbook·Two of four
A Field Guide to Generative AI
02/04
Move 02 · Three metrics, not one

"Accuracy" is a trap.

— Always carry one of each —
WEAK: accuracy = 98% (98% of what? the easy cases?) STRONG: correctness — did it answer? quality — was it on-brand? (1–5) safety — did it leak PII? (zero-tolerance)
One number on an open-ended output almost always hides the failure that matters.

Three metrics — correctness, quality, safety — or you're measuring the easy 80% and ignoring the 20% that breaks you.

The playbook·Three & four
A Field Guide to Generative AI
03/04
Move 03 · Calibrate the judge

Don't trust the robot reviewer.

— If an LLM scores your outputs, check it against a human —
every run: re-score your hand-graded set with the LLM if it disagrees with the human by > 1 point → flag the judge, don't trust the numbers
Move 04 · Different bars

Set the pass bar before you measure — and use a different one per metric. Safety is zero-tolerance. Quality is good-enough. "We'll ship when it's better" is not a bar.
